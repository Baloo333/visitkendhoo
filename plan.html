🧭 Getting there & settling in

Plan Your Visit

Kendhoo is a working local island, not a resort — getting there takes a little more planning, and is part of the experience.

✈️ Getting there

Three ways to reach Kendhoo

Every route starts at Velana International Airport (Malé). Your guesthouse can usually help coordinate the final leg once you've booked.

✈️

Domestic flight + speedboat — fastest

Fly roughly 25 minutes from Malé to Dharavandhoo Airport (several flights daily), then take a speedboat of about 40 minutes on to Kendhoo. The most convenient option if your schedule is tight, and easiest for early arrivals or late departures.

🚤

Direct speedboat from Malé

A more affordable, slower option: speedboat transfers run on a daily schedule, taking roughly 2.5–4 hours depending on conditions and route. Departure times are fixed, so confirm the day's schedule with your guesthouse before booking flights.

⛴️

Public ferry — the budget route

A public inter-island ferry connects Malé to Kendhoo a couple of times a week, taking around 7 hours at a fraction of the speedboat cost — a slow, scenic way to travel between atolls. Schedules change, so check current timings locally before relying on it.

Exact prices and schedules vary by season and operator — your guesthouse will confirm current options and can often arrange the whole transfer for you once your stay is booked.

🏡 Where to stay

Guesthouses on Kendhoo

As an inhabited local island, Kendhoo's accommodation is entirely small, family-run guesthouses rather than resorts — book directly and you're supporting the island's own households.

🤝 Visiting respectfully

Local etiquette

Kendhoo is a conservative, devout Muslim community first, and a destination second. A little awareness goes a long way.

What should I wear in the village?
Cover shoulders and knees on village streets, even on a hot day. Swimwear is fine only at a designated "bikini beach," if the island has one, or once you're out on an excursion boat or sandbank — not while walking through the village.
Can I take photos of people?
Always ask first, especially for portraits of residents or anything near the Magaamfulhu or mosques. Landscapes, harbours and your own group are generally fine.
Is alcohol available?
No — alcohol is prohibited on inhabited local islands like Kendhoo by law, and is only served on resort islands and licensed safari boats. Pork is similarly not available.
What about visiting during Ramadan?
Eating, drinking or smoking in public during daylight hours is discouraged out of respect for those fasting. Evenings, by contrast, come alive with family meals and a warm, festive atmosphere.
Any house rules for homes or mosques?
Remove your shoes before entering a home or mosque, and keep voices low near the Magaamfulhu and Friday Mosque — both are treated by islanders as places of quiet respect, not just sightseeing stops.
What's the best time of year to visit?
For manta rays at Hanifaru Bay, aim for late June through October. For calmer seas generally, the dry season (roughly December–March) is more reliable, though Hanifaru's season runs opposite to it — plan around whichever matters more to you.
