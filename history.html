800 Years of Island Story

Discover the roots of the Maldives on an island where centuries-old traditions are still lived daily.

The Arrival of Islam

Maldivian tradition holds that an Arab scholar — remembered here as Thabreyzgefaanu — who is widely credited with introducing Islam to the Maldives in 1153, stopped on this island.

He asked for a young coconut to drink, found it exceptionally sweet, and renamed the island Fani Kendhoo ("sweet Kendhoo"). The spot where he first prayed was later marked by a coral-stone house, the Magaamfulhu, which still stands today as a testament to this pivotal moment in history.

The Old Mosque

Built from quarried coral stone with finely carved woodwork.

Built from the reef

Kendhoo's historic Mosque is counted among the oldest in Baa Atoll — built, like so much of pre-modern Maldivian architecture, from quarried coral stone with finely carved woodwork inside.

These coral-stone mosques are a hallmark of Maldivian craftsmanship: cool, white-walled, and built to outlast the salt air for centuries.
